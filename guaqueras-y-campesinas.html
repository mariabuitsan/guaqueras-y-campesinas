Guaqueras y Campesinas del occidente de Boyacá

Colección digital sobre las historias y memorias de las mujeres de los municipios del occidente de Boyacá, Colombia.

Digital collection documenting the stories and memories of women from Western Boyacá, Colombia

Claudia Vargas, Martha Ramos, Rubiela Ruiz, Silvia Bernal, Yolima Cruz
Guaqueras is a term most likely derived from the Quechua vocable ‘waca’ or ‘huaca.’ A ‘waca’ denoted sacred or valuable objects as well as the physical locations where these items were placed. Often, wacas were found near ceremonial sites or indigenous tombs, exalting their cultural and spiritual significance. In contemporary usage, ‘Guaqueras’ is a feminine noun that refers to artisan and low-scale diggers and miners. ‘Campesinas’ means women farmers or peasants.
